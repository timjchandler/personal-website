Boids

This is a work in progress.
Boids were developed in the 1980's by Craig Reynolds. They simulate the flocking behaviour that is commonly demonstrated by some birds and shoals of fish. Reynolds' original paper introducing boids may be found here.

Behaviour

Boids will continue to move at a constant speed, only able to change direction. As a group, the boids will move as a flock, with the actions of each individual depending on the actions of the others.
The steering of each individual boid, is dictated by the following rules:

Rules

  • Separation The boid will try to stay more than a predefined minimum distance from other boids.
  • Cohesion The boid will try to move to the middle of the group of boids.
  • AlignmentThe boid will try to match the direction of travel of other boids within the group.
